Alexis Zachariadis

IN REMEMBRANCE OF

an archaeological promenade in Aventicum

Type

Landscape

Location

Avenches, Switzerland

Year

2013

Supervisors

Luca Ortelli
Maria Chiara Barone

The city of Avencitum was during ancient times the capital of Roman Switzerland. Founded in the 1st century A.D., it became highly influencal for the empire shortly thereafter. Today, its remains lay beside the current town of Avenches, itself founded during medieval times. The project proposes a new promenade that connects the different remains and traces from Roman Aventicum, physically as well as emotionally.

The intervention is mainly two-fold: a new distribution is proposed for the agricultural fields within the chosen scope that follows the ancient Roman grid. Thus, the desire to communicate the archaeological Roman urban form is combined with a utilitarian function in maintaining the already existing agricultural activity. The latter however is controlled so as to have different crops in adjacent fields for the complete expression to be as diverse as possible.

Also, a system of stairs and ramps are placed along the traces of the ancient Roman roads acting just north of the agricultural fields. They connect the different levels present on the site, allowing the visitors to relive the site’s rich past at the same time as they experience the vast beauty of the surrounding landscape. The static, constructed, expression of the implemented system acts as a counterpart to the seasonally changing agricultural fields below.

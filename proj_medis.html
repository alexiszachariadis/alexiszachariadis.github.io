Alexis Zachariadis

Blurred Lines

An experimental dance stage on Medborgarplatsen

Type

Cultural, Public

Location

Stockholm, Sweden

Year

2013

Supervisors

Leif Brodersen
Teres Selberg

A stage for experimental dance is placed on Medborgarplatsen. The building’s identity is that of reciprocity. It is in a constant state of motion, of exchange, between the dancer and the audience, between the interior and the exterior, between the building and the square. The roof acts as the extrusion of the building site to mark its location in the existing urban tissue. To provoke vagueness in perception of surface and volume the glass layer of the building stands in contrast to its rectangular, geometrically strict cover with its curved and undulating form.

The curvilinear building consists of two layers. The first one, the outer one, is its urban imprint. It is part of an urbanistic strategy where the form is created to define and enhance the flux of people on the site. The second, inner, layer is the black box. Its shape, an ellipse, is geometrically finite. At the same time, it is endless, allowing it to act as a container of an interminable array of possibilities. It can be seen as an extract of a larger whole, a piece of a grander context. With the simple act of pulling apart the curtains, it merges with the rest of the building.

Together with the vertical elements transcending the boundary between inside and outside, it creates what could be described as a spatial continuum. In this non-hierarchical composition of space, the lines between the square and the building, between private and public, between the dancer and the audience, are blurred and the two opposites fuse in a perpetual state of motion.
